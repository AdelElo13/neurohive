## 1. Donation & Zakat Platforms

### LaunchGood

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **What it does** | Crowdfunding platform for Muslim causes (campaigns, emergencies, zakat, sadaqah) |
| **Scale** | $688M+ raised, 2.1M donors, 155 countries |
| **Revenue model** | 0% platform fee; relies on optional donor tips (70%+ tip); paid marketing/promotion packages for charities (25-50% commission via "algorithm") |
| **What's good** | Largest Muslim crowdfunding platform; easy campaign creation; built-in zakat calculator; global reach |
| **What's BAD** | Charities must pay for visibility (marketing fees of $3K+ on $19K raised); only ~68% of donations may reach intended cause when fees stack; vetting of campaigns is weak despite claims; withdrawal delays (3+ weeks reported); blocks Indian citizens entirely; zakat add-on fees may contradict their own zakat policy; Trustpilot complaints about fraud and closed support tickets |

### MyTenNights

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **What it does** | Automates donations across last 10 nights of Ramadan so donors never miss Laylatul Qadr |
| **Scale** | 100,000+ donors; raised £10.9M in a single Ramadan |
| **Revenue model** | Platform fee / charity partnerships |
| **What's good** | Brilliant niche concept; timezone-aware auto-scheduling (after Maghrib, before Fajr); donors split between zakat and sadaqah; choose odd nights vs equal split; partners with major charities (Islamic Relief, Penny Appeal, Oxfam) |
| **What's BAD** | Single-purpose (Ramadan only); no year-round engagement; limited to partner charities |

### GlobalSadaqah

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **What it does** | CSR, Zakat, and Waqf management platform (Malaysia-based) |
| **Scale** | 5x donor growth in 2021; partners with banks, foundations, religious bodies |
| **Revenue model** | B2B partnerships with institutions and corporates |
| **What's good** | Award-winning; supports crypto donations (via SINEGY/LuxTag); wide payment options (FPX, e-wallets, Apple/Google Pay, PayPal); Shariah-compliant vetting |
| **What's BAD** | Malaysia-centric; limited consumer brand awareness outside Southeast Asia; primarily institutional rather than grassroots |

### National Zakat Foundation (NZF)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **What it does** | UK's only nonprofit dedicated exclusively to zakat collection and distribution |
| **Scale** | £28M+ allocated since 2011; 43,000 people helped |
| **Revenue model** | Nonprofit; zakat admin allocation |
| **What's good** | ZakaTracker tool for donor transparency; £1 zakat = £73 societal return; 80% faster processing via Microsoft automation; three clear fund categories (Hardship Relief, Housing & Work, Education) |
| **What's BAD** | UK-focused only; wait times can be 2+ months during peak; mixed Trustpilot reviews on response speed; generic automated emails |

### Major Charity Organizations (Islamic Relief, Penny Appeal, Muslim Aid, MATW Project)

| Organization | Scale | Revenue Model | Key Strengths | Key Weaknesses |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Islamic Relief USA** | 40+ countries | Donations, grants | Established brand, zakat calculator, wide programs | Criticized for administrative bloat; new zakat policy allows 20% admin fees (up from claimed 12.5%) |
| **Penny Appeal** | 30+ countries | Donations, MyTenNights partnership | 89% to programs; automated Ramadan giving; aqiqah services | Lower brand recognition than Islamic Relief; limited tech/app experience |
| **Muslim Aid** | 70+ countries | Donations | Wide geographic reach | Less transparency data available; legacy platform |
| **MATW Project** | 30 countries, 19M+ reached | 100% donation policy | Founded by Ali Banat's personal story; emotional brand; 100% policy | Relies on separate funding for admin; independent verification scarce |
| **SKT Welfare** | 30+ countries | 100% donation policy | Every penny to projects | Less well-known; limited digital infrastructure |

### Donorbox (Islamic vertical)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **What it does** | General fundraising platform with Islamic-specific features |
| **Revenue model** | SaaS fees to organizations |

## 6. Trust & Transparency Crisis

### The Core Problem

The Muslim charitable sector faces a documented, systemic trust crisis. This is the single biggest opportunity in the space.

### Key Findings from the Ehsan/Ahmed Shaikh Zakat Report (March 2026)

- **Nearly 60% of money** donated to some organizations is either "burned or hoarded"
- Islamic scholars and public figures routinely endorse low-quality and scammy organizations
- Some organizations have **no zakat rules or accountability** while collecting millions
- One organization gave **28.7% of LaunchGood zakat campaign donations** to Khaled Beydoun as an influencer commission
- Organizations claim "100% Zakat Policies" but financial filings show **no segregated restricted funds** and no articulated zakat policies
- Islamic Relief USA raised admin fees to 20%, replacing a previous 12.5% rate that was described as fabricated
- CAIR-National accused of misleading Muslims on zakat
- Best-in-class example: **Hidaya** — ~$89 of every $100 donated reaches grantees

### Structural Vulnerabilities

- **Cash-heavy ecosystem**: Significant zakat/sadaqah collected in cash at mosques and community events, making verifiable audit trails nearly impossible
- **Informal transfer systems**: Many disbursements rely on hawala-like networks lacking banking transparency
- **Fund commingling**: Risk of zakat funds being mixed with general donations or illicit proceeds
- **FATF warning (2023)**: Abuse of zakat and charitable flows for illicit ends remains a pressing regulatory risk

### LaunchGood-Specific Trust Issues

- "Unique algorithm" for charity promotion effectively amounts to 25-50% sales commission
- Charities must pay to be visible; organic discovery is suppressed
- Zakat add-on fees contradict stated zakat policy
- Weak campaign vetting despite claims of "rigorous vetting"
- Geographic discrimination (India blocked)
- Withdrawal delays and unresponsive support

### Muslim Pro Privacy Scandal

- Sold user location data to X-Mode, purchased by US military
- 170M+ Muslim users' location data compromised
- Lawsuits filed in France for data protection offences, abuse of trust, and "endangering lives"
- Fourth Amendment loophole: government surveillance of Muslim community via commercial data purchase
- Company claimed it was "incorrect and untrue" then immediately terminated all data partnerships

## 7. What Does NOT Exist Yet

Based on community discussions, Reddit threads, and market analysis, here is what Muslims want but cannot find:

### 1. Transparent Donation Tracking (End-to-End)
No app lets a donor follow their specific zakat dollar from payment to recipient with verifiable proof. Blockchain prototypes exist academically but no consumer product has shipped at scale.

### 2. Unified Muslim "Financial + Spiritual" App
Nothing combines: zakat calculation + payment + tracking + halal investing + waqf/sadaqah jariyah + mosque donations in one place. These are all separate apps with no interoperability.

### 3. Local Muslim Community Platform
No "Nextdoor for Muslims" exists — finding local halaqas, Quran teachers with credentials, Muslim parents groups, halal grocery group buys, community events. CMZ and ConnectMazjid are mosque-centric, not community-centric.

### 4. Mosque Discovery with Real Information
Users want: accessibility info (ramps, women's sections, baby facilities), user-uploaded photos, parking details, real-time prayer times (not just calculated), khutbah language, community demographics. Nothing provides this comprehensively.

### 5. Privacy-First Muslim Super App
After the Muslim Pro scandal, there is massive demand for a comprehensive Islamic app that explicitly does not collect or sell user data. Pillars proves the demand but is feature-limited.

### 6. Verified Charity Rating System for Islamic Orgs
No "Charity Navigator for Muslim charities" exists. No independent, community-trusted system rates Islamic charities on zakat compliance, transparency, overhead ratios, and actual impact.

### 7. Sadaqah Jariyah Investment Platform
No consumer platform lets ordinary Muslims invest small amounts into waqf/sadaqah jariyah with transparent returns reporting and verified impact. The International Waqf Fund and Waqf Fund exist but are traditional, not tech-first.

### 8. AI-Powered Islamic Financial Advisor
No app combines: "Is this halal?" stock screening + zakat calculation on complex portfolios (crypto, real estate, business assets) + personalized giving recommendations + tax optimization for charitable deductions.

### 9. Muslim Mental Health & Counseling Platform
Despite growing awareness, no dedicated platform connects Muslims with faith-sensitive therapists, Islamic counseling resources, and community support in a stigma-free digital environment.

### 10. Cross-Border Zakat Distribution Network
No platform efficiently handles the "I'm in the US but my family in Yemen/Somalia/Syria needs zakat" use case with transparency, compliance, and speed.

## 8. Market Gaps & Opportunities

### The Big Picture

| Market Segment | Size | Current State | Opportunity Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Global Muslim population | 2B+ people | Fragmented app ecosystem | Massive |
| Annual global zakat obligation | $200B-$1T estimated | Most uncollected; collected portion poorly tracked | Enormous |
| Islamic fintech market | 375+ startups tracked | Growing but fragmented | High |
| Global Islamic edtech | $6B+ market | AI-driven growth (Tarteel leading) | High |
| Halal economy overall | $2.3T+ | Digital transformation early stage | Massive |

### Top Opportunity Themes

#### 1. TRUST INFRASTRUCTURE (Highest Priority)
The single biggest pain point is: "Where does my money actually go?" A platform that solves donation transparency with verifiable tracking, independent charity ratings, and real-time impact reporting would capture massive market share. This is an infrastructure play, not just an app.

#### 2. CONSOLIDATION PLAY
The Muslim app ecosystem is absurdly fragmented: separate apps for prayer, Quran, donations, halal food, mosque finding, investing, community. A privacy-first super app that does 5-6 of these well (not 15 poorly like Muslim Pro) could dominate.

#### 3. LOCAL-FIRST COMMUNITY
The gap between "global charity platform" and "my local mosque" is enormous. A hyperlocal Muslim community platform (events, services, teachers, group buys, mutual aid) with mosque integration would be novel.

#### 4. SMART GIVING ENGINE
Combine: zakat calculator for complex assets + automated distribution across verified charities + tax receipt generation + impact tracking + Ramadan automation (like MyTenNights but year-round) + sadaqah jariyah investment. No single product does this.

#### 5. B2B MOSQUE INFRASTRUCTURE
Mosques are underserved with technology. A modern, free/freemium mosque management platform that includes: donation processing, community app, event management, transparent financial reporting to congregation, volunteer coordination — with a business model based on payment processing margin rather than SaaS fees.

### Competitive Moats to Build

1. **Trust/transparency** — verified charity ratings, blockchain tracking, public financial data
2. **Privacy** — zero data selling, minimal data collection, end-to-end encryption
3. **Community network effects** — local Muslim communities are sticky once engaged
4. **Scholarly endorsement** — partnerships with trusted Islamic scholars for legitimacy
5. **Regulatory compliance** — proper charity regulation, financial services licensing

## Summary

The Islamic app and donation landscape in 2026 is:

- **Large**: 2B+ Muslims, $200B+ in annual zakat obligations, $2.3T halal economy
- **Fragmented**: Dozens of single-purpose apps, no dominant platform
- **Trust-broken**: Documented fraud, opaque charity finances, data selling scandals
- **Technically behind**: Most platforms are basic web forms; little AI, blockchain, or modern UX
- **Ripe for disruption**: The combination of trust crisis + fragmentation + growing Muslim tech-savvy population + massive underserved financial flows creates a generational opportunity

The winning play is likely a **trust-first, privacy-first platform** that starts with one wedge (donation transparency or smart zakat) and expands into adjacent services (community, education, finance) with a credible, verifiable approach to where money goes.
